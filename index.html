The Season of Giving at IIT Kanpur is a heartfelt celebration of generosity, community, and the shared journey of creating a lasting impact. Each contribution, no matter the size, builds a stronger, brighter future for your alma mater and reinforces your role as a partner in its journey forward. Join the season’s spirit and help shape a promising future for IIT Kanpur.

Stories of Change

Aniket comes from a humble family where money was always short. Almost half of his family’s annual income would have gone into hostel and mess charges at IIT Kanpur, making it difficult for him to continue. Despite this, he worked hard and kept his dream alive. Your support helped him cover these essential costs, allowing him to focus on academics and his passion for space and defense research. He now hopes to serve the nation through ISRO or DRDO and one day support other students like him.

Aniket Shinde

Sneh comes from a small village where her father drives an auto-rickshaw and her mother manages the home. As an EWS student, financial struggles constantly stood in the way of her education, making the dream of IIT Kanpur seem far away. Paying for basic needs like hostel and electricity was a constant worry. Your support not only lifted this weight but also gave her the confidence to dream bigger. Inspired by this kindness, Sneh hopes to one day pay it forward and support students from villages like hers.

Sneh Omer

Anshika comes from a small village, in a family dependent on farming as the only source of income. Covering essential expenses like mess and electricity bills at IIT Kanpur was a heavy burden, and at one point her ability to continue studies and prepare for placements was at risk. Alumni support lifted that pressure, giving her the space to focus on academics and her future. Today, she dreams of succeeding in her career and helping other young women from villages like hers.

Anshika Singh

Vidhi grew up in a small city in Rajasthan, in a joint family where finances were always tight. During her JEE preparation, both her parents were hospitalized, leaving her with the fear of not being able to continue her studies. Even after joining IIT Kanpur, essential expenses added pressure to her family. Your support lifted that burden, giving Vidhi the chance to excel, earning awards, securing internships, and even a pre-placement offer. She now aspires to pursue higher studies and inspire others from small towns like hers.

Vidhi Jain

Amit is the son of a farmer from a small village where the family depended on agriculture as the only source of income. The seasonal nature of farming meant his parents constantly worried about managing household needs, let alone the costs of IIT Kanpur. Covering hostel and living expenses often felt impossible. Alumni support eased this struggle, allowing Amit to continue his studies with peace of mind. He now dreams of building a successful career and supporting students from farming families like his.

Amit Kumar

Harsh’s father, the primary breadwinner, worked long hours on the farm, while his mother stitched clothes to add to the family income. Despite these struggles, Harsh not only managed his academics at IIT Kanpur but also pursued his love for sports like football and badminton, which taught him teamwork and resilience. Your support allowed him to continue his education without the crushing financial burden, and he now dreams of excelling in both his career and life, while giving back to students like him.

Harsh Wardhan Singh Shekhawat

Amipriya faced hardships early in life when he lost a parent, leaving his father, an auto-rickshaw driver, as the only earner in the family. With such limited income, higher education seemed almost impossible. Reaching IIT Kanpur was his biggest dream, but covering mess and living expenses became a huge challenge. Alumni support gave him the relief he needed to continue his studies without burdening his family. Today, he is motivated to succeed and hopes to give back by helping other students facing similar struggles.

Amipriya Anand

Voices of Generosity

My career would not be possible without the formative years I spent at IITK. The knowledge I gained there in academics and the interaction with peers gave me a critical foundation. I'm especially thankful for the outstanding faculty at IITK, who were so patient and generous with their time. They instilled in me intellectual tools that I continue to use today, like the ability to break a complex problem into its components and to reason logically and statistically. There was also the crucible of debating with classmates, which honed my critical thinking. I have a profound sense of gratitude for this great institution which has played such a seminal role in shaping my worldview.

Dr. Arvind Krishna
(BT/EE/1985) Chairman & Chief Executive Officer, IBM

My journey at IIT Kanpur, where I graduated with a B.Tech in Computer Science in 1995, was transformative in ways that continue to resonate nearly three decades later. The rigorous academic environment not only equipped me with technical expertise but also instilled a problem-solving mindset that has proven invaluable throughout my career. What truly sets IITK apart is its unique ecosystem that nurtures both intellectual growth and lasting relationships. The friendships forged in the hostels, during late-night study and bulla sessions, and through Culfests, movie screenings, and other cultural activities have stood the test of time. Contributing to IITK is more than just giving back – it's an investment in preserving and enhancing this extraordinary environment for future generations. Every time I hear about the achievements of current students or witness the innovative technologies emerging from our campus, I'm reminded of why supporting IITK matters. The skills, values,and relationships I developed at IITK have been cornerstone elements of my life and career. Supporting the institution through giving is my way of ensuring that future students have access to the same transformative experiences that shaped my journey.

Mr. Gokul Rajaram
(BT/CS/1992) CTO, TechNext Innovations

I arrived on the campus of IIT Kanpur in 1971 as a 16-year old, proud of having qualified with the high rank of All India 13. I was both excited and nervous to be embarking on the non- traditional journey of becoming an engineer in this very male dominated environment. These were the early days of IITK. Only two women had graduated B.Tech – Kavery Muthana, the director's daughter, and Neeru Mohan, my cousin and inspiration. I graduated with B.Tech in EE in 1976, and then M.Tech in EE in 1979. The education was excellent, with a very accomplished faculty and bright classmates. Above all, IIT taught me how to thrive in a male world. I also have very warm memories of so many fun times spent with friends, especially in the Girls' Hostel. IIT opened the doors of the world to me. After IIT, I pursued higher education at Carnegie-Mellon University in the USA, and worked at Philips Research. I have had a rewarding career as an engineer and researcher that has brought a sense of accomplishment. I am proud to have graduated from IIT Kanpur. Even after all these years, I can impress people just by telling them that I graduated from IITK. For the past many years, I, along with my husband, have been making annual contributions to the IITK Foundation as part of its annual giving program. We feel it is important to give back to an institution that has given me so much, and to which I am still deeply attached. Our donations allow IITK to keep investing in facilities and students, and to reach new heights. As an alumna, I feel invested in IIT's future. I want to continue being proud of having graduated from IITK.

Ms. Roli Garg Wendorf
(BT/ME/2000) Founder & CEO, MechaLabs

My five years as a B.Tech student from 1977 to 1982 provided a strong foundation for my subsequent achievements. One enduring memory is of my batchmate during the first three years, who, despite coming from a humble rural background, cleared the JEE through his own efforts. Fortunately, tuition and hostel fees were very nominal at that time. With a merit cum means scholarship, he graduated with good grades and is now well-settled abroad. Today, tuition fees and other expenses are beyond the reach of many deserving students. Helping such students is my primary motivation to give back to IITK.

Mr. Dhanraj K. Chokappa
(BT/ME/2000) Founder & CEO, MechaLabs
